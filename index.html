Description

In Hamachi the Psychotic Killer, you play as a piece of tofu named Hamachi who unexpectedly falls into the clutches of Demon Queen Deity, an evil fiend who intends to use him as a vessel to conquer the world through mass murder. Decide the fate of everyone you meet: kill, spare, or ignore them entirely. Your actions unfold into three distinct routes: Pacifist, Neutral, and Genocide, each with its own unique ending.

Killing attracts the police, turning your first encounter with the law into a high-stakes chase. You’ll encounter and battle two demonic bosses, or you can show them you mean no harm and pacify them, for which they’ll join your party, bringing their assistance in the final battle on a True Pacifist route.

Hamachi can occasionally wield himself with a chainsaw power up called the “Psycho Saw”, that grants temporary invincibility, a speed boost, and allows you to destroy any being you touch.

Throughout 8 visually distinct worlds, Hamachi can collect the souls from dead civilians, or collect sushi delivered by a “supporting” character named Penelope Pom Pom Pink to restore health.

Hamachi the Psychotic Killer is a 2D platformer that lets you embrace the role of the villain.

Our Story

Hamachi the Psychotic Killer is created by Gavin O'Riley, an independent developer based in Los Angeles, USA. What started as an idea — “Normally you always play as the hero in platformer games, what if the roles were reversed and you were the bad guy?” — has grown into a full-fledged passion project packed with branching paths, recruitable bosses, and plenty of gore (or surprisingly wholesome moments).

Built almost entirely by one person, with occasional help from talented freelancers for music and additional art, the game is a love letter to classic platformers, morality systems, and over-the-top villainy.
